Home

TSM (Team SoloMid) is one of North America's oldest and most well-known eSports brands. Since their creation in 2011, TSM has qualified for every Worlds Championship, and has never failed to place top two in North America. However, North America has always been regarded internationally as one of the weaker regions in League of Legends, never making it far on the world stage. This year's TSM roster is looking like the best team North America has ever produced, coming off of an absolutely dominating 17-1 record during the regular season. This could be North America's biggest chance to finally bring home the World Cup.

Roster

Kevin "Hauntzer" Yarnell


Kevin "Hauntzer" Yarnell joined the revamped TSM roster as a relative rookie compared to the rest of TSM's star-studded roster. Despite coming highly recommended at the hands of Marcus "Dyrus" Hill after the long-time TSM top laner's retirement, Hauntzer was a relatively large question mark on the TSM roster. However, it only took a few games for the former Gravity/Curse Academy top laner to ease the fans' fears, displaying considerable aptitude with the low-econ style Dyrus was known for. However, Hauntzer did not stop there, going on to showcase his versatility and carry potential as well. Although content to receive few resources and scale up as a teamfight threat, Hauntzer is also very capable of running away with a lead - one such example was in Spring Split 2016, where Hauntzer's Poppy absolutely demolished worlds semifinalist Huni's Fiora.
Hauntzer continued to improve furiously through the Spring Split, and became arguably the best overall top laner in North America during summer. Hauntzer's continued improvement will be pivotal in TSM's worlds run, as this will be his first encounter against truly world-class top laners such as Smeb, who many agree has unthroned Faker as the best League of Legends player in the world.
